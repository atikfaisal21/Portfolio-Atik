Welcome

Exploring Materials Science Through Innovation and Research

As a final-year Materials and Metallurgical Engineering student at BUET, I am driven by the challenge of bridging computational theory with experimental innovation to accelerate materials discovery. My research trajectory demonstrates this vision: I have worked in an optical-image-based methodology for rapid layer detection that replaces resource-intensive Atomic Force Microscopy and electron microscopy with Python-driven computational analysis—work now published in Computational Materials Science. My research spans nanomaterials synthesis (co-precipitation, hydrothermal methods), advanced thin-film characterization (XRD, SEM, AFM), physical metallurgy, and DFT simulations. Currently I am investigating valleytronic materials through DFT+U simulations with spin-orbit coupling to design heterostructures for next-generation electronics. Simultaneously, my work on SnO₂ thin films for photodiode applications demonstrates my commitment to translating fundamental materials physics into functional devices. What distinguishes me is not innate talent, but disciplined persistence and intellectual curiosity. I approach setbacks as learning opportunities and systematically iterate on both experimental protocols and computational models. I am particularly drawn to PhD programs where I can deepen my expertise in computational materials science while maintaining strong experimental foundations, ultimately contributing to materials innovation in energy storage, electronics, and photocatalysis.

About Me

With a strong foundation in materials characterization, simulation techniques, and computational materials science, I am committed to pushing the boundaries of materials engineering. My research interests span composite materials, thin film deposition, metallurgical defects analysis, and optoelectronic material design, nanoparticle synthesis with rare earth doping.

I leverage cutting-edge tools like DFT simulations, machine learning models, and advanced characterization techniques (SEM, UV-Vis spectroscopy, XRD) to predict material properties and discover novel materials for industrial applications.

Currently preparing PhD applications in the USA, I am eager to contribute to the field through groundbreaking research and innovative solutions to global materials challenges.

Current Projects

Valleytronics

2H-TaSe₂ was investigated as a valleytronic material through DFT-based simulations incorporating spin–orbit coupling (SOC) and DFT+U corrections. A three-step workflow (geometry relaxation, SOC-enabled static run, and non-SCF band structure calculation) was optimized to study valley polarization induced via magnetic proximity effects. The study establishes TaSe₂ as a valley-active 2D layer, suitable for integration with ferromagnetic substrates such as *** in future valleytronic heterostructures. Current Status: Looking for better match of the materials. This work is under the supervision of Dr. Saquib Ahmed

DFT Machine Learning Optoelectronics

Thin Film deposition of SnO2

Deposition of SnO₂ thin films on glass substrates with specific amounts of dopants is carried out to investigate the conductive properties of the films. This coating is implemented for photodiode applications. The work establishes a Schottky contact between pure SnO₂ and doped SnO₂ films to induce photoconductivity. The film also addresses the drawbacks associated with persistent photoconductivity. Current Status: Experiments are done. Characterizations are also done. Manuscript preparation is ongoing. This work is under the Supervision of Dr. Azizar Rahman

DFT Thin Film Semi-Conductors

Antibiotic Removal by NanoParticles

This work investigates the TiO2 and ZnO nanoparticle synthesis through hydrothermal method. TiO2 and ZnO nanoparticles are employed to study the antibiotic removal from wastewater. The photocatalysts yield excellent results regarding the removal of mixture of antibiotics from wastewater. Current Status: Manuscript writing is completed. Submission to journal's are on the way. This work is under the supervision of Dr. Md. Abdul Gafur
